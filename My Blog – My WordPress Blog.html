Chronicles of Exoplanet Exploration

“Discover the captivating world of exoplanets and unlock the mysteries of the universe, one planet at a time!”

WHAT ARE EXOPLANETS ?

An exoplanet is any planet beyond our solar system. Most of them orbit other stars, but some free-floating exoplanets, called rogue planets, are untethered to any star. We’ve confirmed more than 5,600 exoplanets out of the billions that we believe exist.

DISCOVERY OF EXOPLANETS

First exoplanets discovered
Aleksander Wolszczan and Dale Frail announce the discovery of two rocky planets orbiting PSR B1 257+12, a pulsar in the constellation Virgo. Because they are constantly bombarded by radiation from the dead neutron star that they orbit, these rocky planets cannot support organic life.

FEATURED SECTION OF EXOPLANETS

Proxima Centauri b: The closest exoplanet to Earth, potentially in the habitable zone.
Kepler-22b: A “super-Earth” in the habitable zone of its star.
TRAPPIST-1e: One of seven Earth-sized planets in the TRAPPIST-1 system, with potential for water.
HD 209458 b (Osiris): A gas giant losing its atmosphere due to its star’s heat.
55 Cancri e: A rocky planet possibly covered in lava, with extreme temperatures.

TYPES OF EXOPLANETS

6 POPULAR EXPOPLANETS

1. Proxima Centauri b

Type: Rocky Exoplanet

Distance from Earth: 4.2 light-years

Description: Proxima Centauri b is the closest known exoplanet to Earth and orbits in the habitable zone of its star, Proxima Centauri. It is a rocky planet with the potential for liquid water on its surface, making it a candidate for hosting life.

4. HD 209458 b (Osiris)

Type: Hot Jupiter

Distance from Earth: 159 light-years

Description: HD 209458 b, also known as Osiris, is a hot Jupiter that orbits very close to its star. It is known for its atmospheric loss due to the intense heat from its star, leading to a unique cloud and storm system.

2. Kepler-22b

Type: Super-Earth

Distance from Earth: 600 light-years

Description: Kepler-22b is a super-Earth located in the habitable zone of a Sun-like star. It is approximately 2.4 times the size of Earth and has the potential for liquid water.

5. 55 Cancri e

Type: Super-Earth (Carbon Planet)

Distance from Earth: 40 light-years

Description: 55 Cancri e is a super-Earth that is believed to be composed largely of carbon, including a possible diamond core. It is extremely hot, with surface temperatures high enough to melt metal.

3. TRAPPIST-1e

Type: Earth-sized Exoplanet

Distance from Earth: 40 light-years

Description: TRAPPIST-1e is one of the seven Earth-sized planets in the TRAPPIST-1 system, located in the habitable zone. It has a similar size to Earth and may have liquid

6. WASP-121b

Type: Hot Jupiter

Distance from Earth: 900 light-years

Description: WASP-121b is a tidally locked hot Jupiter with extreme temperatures exceeding 2,500°C (4,532°F). It has a thick atmosphere rich in heavy metals like magnesium and iron, leading to unique weather patterns, including metal clouds

FUTURE PLANS FOR EXOPLANET EXPLORATION

Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope
Expected Launch: Late 2020s
Description: This mission will focus on wide-field imaging and exoplanet surveys. It will employ the microlensing technique to discover new exoplanets, especially those that are Earth-sized and located in the habitable zone. The Roman Space Telescope will also assist in studying the distribution of exoplanets across the Milky Way galaxy.
European Space Agency’s ARIEL Mission
Launch Date: Scheduled for 2029
Description: The Atmospheric Remote-Sensing Infrared Exoplanet Large-survey (ARIEL) mission will study the atmospheres of a diverse range of exoplanets. By measuring the composition of gases and identifying chemical signatures, ARIEL aims to provide insights into planetary formation and the potential for habitability.
Innovative Technologies
Direct Imaging Techniques: Advanced imaging techniques, including star shades and coronagraphs, are being developed to block out starlight and directly observe exoplanets. This will help scientists study their surfaces and atmospheres in unprecedented detail.
Machine Learning and AI: These technologies are increasingly used to analyze vast datasets from telescopes, helping researchers identify patterns and detect exoplanets more efficiently.
These upcoming missions and technologies promise to revolutionize our understanding of exoplanets and their potential for hosting life. By exploring distant worlds, we are not only expanding our knowledge of the universe but also answering fundamental questions about our place within it.
